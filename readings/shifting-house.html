My Website is a Shifting House — Laurel Schwulst

  • a website is a file or bundle of files living on a server somewhere

  • links are the oxygen of the web

  • on the internet no one is looking out for you, but you

  • websites are both subject and object at once — creator is author and architect

  • the web's vitality depends on a interconnected web of individual nodes breathing life into a vast network

  • "how could a website complement what you already do rather than competing or repeating?"

  • technology is the active human interface with the material world

  • a website can be:
    (1) a room → comforting, finite, flexible
    (2) a shelf → simple, easy to change out objects
    (3) a plant → not to be rushed, grows on its own
    (4) a garden → changes with seasons, balanced
    (5) a puddle → temporary collection, can happen in a burst, no "completedness," evaporates overtime
    (6) a thrown rock that's now falling deep into the ocean → doesn't need to be maintained, "when an idea comes, find a rock and throw it"

  • the internet is not a cloud--not mysterious or untouchable, not one thing, made up of individual nodes

  • the web is a flock of birds

  • it is instrumental that websites progress along with us
